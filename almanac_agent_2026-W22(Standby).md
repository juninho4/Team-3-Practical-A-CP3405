## Focus Week

Late May / Early June 2026

This Almanac analysis focuses on the seasonal background for the next market prediction week. It does not make the final team prediction by itself. It only gives the historical and seasonal bias that will later be combined with macro, technical, LLM, and human judgement.

## Monthly Context

The market is moving from late May into early June. According to the Stock Trader's Almanac 2026, this period should be treated carefully because May starts the weaker seasonal half of the year, and 2026 is a midterm election year.

The Almanac also describes Q2 and Q3 of midterm election years as a weaker part of the four-year presidential cycle. This means the seasonal background is not strongly bullish, even if short-term charts look positive.

## Late May / Memorial Day Pattern

The late-May period is also affected by Memorial Day seasonal behaviour. The Almanac notes that Memorial Day week has produced negative Dow performance in 17 of the last 29 years, suggesting a weaker recent seasonal tendency.

This supports a cautious view for the transition from May into June. It also suggests that low-volume holiday trading may make the market less reliable as a signal.

## Sector Seasonality

The Almanac sector seasonality table shows both positive and negative sector tendencies around this period.

### Positive / Supportive Seasonal Areas

- Information Technology has a positive seasonal window from mid-March to mid-July.
- Computer Technology has a positive seasonal window from mid-April to mid-July.
- Utilities also have a positive seasonal window from mid-March to early October.

These signals may support NASDAQ or large-cap technology if market conditions remain risk-on; this seasonal strength partially offsets the broader bearish June seasonality signal.

### Negative / Weak Seasonal Areas

- Banking has a short seasonal window from early May to early July.
- Gold & Silver has a short seasonal window from mid-May to late June.
- Materials has a short seasonal window from mid-May to mid-October.
- Oil begins a negative seasonal window in early June.
- Natural Gas turns negative from mid-June to late July.

This means the sector picture is mixed: technology may provide support, but financials, materials, energy-related areas, and metals may create pressure.

## Almanac Thesis

The Almanac evidence suggests that the next trading week should be approached with caution because the market is moving from May into June during a midterm election year, and June has historically been weak for Dow, S&P 500, NASDAQ, and Russell 2000 in midterm years.

However, the signal is not fully bearish because technology-related sectors still have positive seasonal support into July. Therefore, the most reasonable Almanac view is cautious bearish-neutral for the broad market, with NASDAQ potentially more resilient than SPX and IWM if technology leadership continues.

The team should avoid making a high-confidence bullish prediction unless macro and technical evidence strongly confirm it.
