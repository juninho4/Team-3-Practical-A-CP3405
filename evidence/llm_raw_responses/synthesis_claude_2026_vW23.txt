However, this is offset by the geopolitical source of the oil risk and the counteracting pressure of a stronger dollar and rising long yields.

5. Invalidation Conditions:
The bearish view would materially weaken under any combination of the following: SPX reclaims and closes above 7,500 on meaningful volume, suggesting buyers have returned at scale; NDX recovers above 29,500, reversing the breakdown in technology momentum; a credible ceasefire or de-escalation agreement between the US and Iran removes the geopolitical risk premium from oil and shipping lanes; the 10-year yield reverses and moves sustainably back below 4.30%, reducing the pressure on growth and rate-sensitive equities; or the ECB decision on 11 June delivers an unexpectedly dovish signal that lifts global risk sentiment.
