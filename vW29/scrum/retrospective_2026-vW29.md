## What We Learned About Team Process

-Clear communication at the beginning and throughout the sprint is essential for keeping everyone aligned. Team members should provide regular progress updates without waiting to be asked, allowing blockers to be identified earlier.

-Consistent visibility of completed work helps the Scrum Master monitor sprint progress more accurately and reduces unnecessary follow-up messages. When every role reports completion promptly, the team can make better decisions and maintain momentum.

-Planning role transitions before the final assessment is equally important. Early discussion gives team members enough time to understand their new responsibilities, complete knowledge transfer, and reduce risks during the next sprint.


## Improvements for Next Sprint

-Require every role to provide at least one progress update during the sprint.
-Follow up immediately if no update is received within the agreed reporting period.
-Finalise role switching at the beginning of the sprint and clearly document new responsibilities.
-Improve task visibility by updating GitHub and Discord regularly.
-Encourage members to report completed work proactively instead of waiting to be asked.
