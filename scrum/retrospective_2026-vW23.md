# Retrospective 2026-W23

## Role

R2 Scrum Master

## Purpose

This file records the team retrospective for Week 23. It focuses on Scrum process, team coordination, role accountability, blockers, and improvement actions.

This file focuses on team process. R10's learning log focuses on prediction quality and calibration.

---

## What Went Well

R8 completed their work early. The market data and evidence screenshots were uploaded by midday on the day the data became available, which gave the team a useful starting point.

R4 also completed the Macro / News Agent output on the same evening, so the macro evidence was available early enough for the synthesis stage.

R3 completed the Almanac Agent output on the second day afternoon.

R6 and R7 completed their parts normally. R6 handled the LLM synthesis process, and R7 completed the human judgement work.

The team was also able to keep the sprint moving by preparing a simplified R5 technical section when the original R5 work was not completed in time.

---

## What Did Not Go Well

This week was weaker than last week in terms of process and role accountability.

Although the role structure was already clearer than before, the team did not show enough improvement. Some roles still did not complete their responsibilities during the active sprint window.

R5, R10, and R2 did not contribute on time during the sprint. This meant that other members had to cover missing work so the project could continue.

The sprint only lasted about two working days, so any delay had a large impact. When some role outputs were late or missing, the team had less time to check quality and organise files properly.

The main issue was not lack of task clarity, but lack of follow-through after roles had already been assigned.

---

## Blockers

- Some members were absent or did not complete their assigned role work on time.
- R5 technical analysis was not completed by the assigned role owner.
- R10 logs were not completed during the active sprint period.
- R2 process documentation was delayed.
- The short two-day working window made delays more serious.
- Other members had to take on extra work to prevent the sprint from stopping.

---

## How We Responded

The active members continued working with the evidence that was available.

R8 uploaded the data early, and R4 completed the macro file on the first evening. R3 submitted the Almanac file the next afternoon. R6 and R7 then used the available evidence to complete their tasks.

To avoid blocking the R6 synthesis process, a simplified R5 technical section was prepared using the available market evidence. This allowed the shared prompt and LLM comparison to move forward.

However, this was only a temporary solution. It should not become the normal process, because each role should complete its own responsibility.

---

## What We Learned About Team Process

This sprint showed that simply assigning roles is not enough.

Compared with last week, the team understood the role structure better, but execution did not improve. In some ways, the process slightly regressed because several roles were still not completed on time.

The team learned that role accountability must be stronger. If a role owner is unavailable or delayed, the team needs to know earlier, not near the end of the sprint.

The team also learned that short sprints require earlier check-ins. Waiting until the second day creates too much pressure and reduces the quality of final work.

---

## Improvements for Next Sprint

Next sprint, the team should:

- Confirm at the beginning who is available and who is not.
- Set an earlier internal deadline for each role.
- Require each role to give a short Discord progress update.
- Identify missing roles earlier instead of waiting until the end.
- Avoid relying on other members to cover unfinished work at the last moment.
- Keep R2 process documentation updated during the sprint, not after the sprint.
- Make R10 logs a required final check before submission.
- Make sure R5 technical evidence is completed by the assigned role owner.
