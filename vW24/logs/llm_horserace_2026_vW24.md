## What the Models Missed
Despite the bullish outcome, the models likely missed several key dynamics:

The magnitude of the small-cap rally: IWM gained +3.89% in a single week. Most bullish forecasts would have predicted 1-2% at most.

The sector rotation signal: The Philadelphia semiconductor index surged +7.91% — this wasn't just a broad rally but a sharp rotation into previously beaten-down tech and cyclical names.

The oil/rates catalyst: Lower oil prices and reduced Fed hike expectations were the primary drivers. Models that didn't track commodity-Fed interactions would have missed this.

The SpaceX IPO effect: Markets were also influenced by attention on SpaceX's market debut, an idiosyncratic event that most macro-focused models wouldn't capture.

The mid-week dip and recovery: Markets experienced a significant downturn mid-week (June 11) before recovering. Models that only predicted end-of-week outcomes would have missed the intra-week volatility pattern.

## Reflection
The best-performing model this week was ChatGPT — a complete reversal from Week 23, where ChatGPT was the worst performer.

The weakest-performing model this week was DeepSeek — also a reversal from Week 23, where DeepSeek was the best.

Key insight for the team: Week 23 was bearish, Week 24 was bullish. A model's performance flipped entirely based on regime direction. This suggests that:

No single model has a permanent edge. Regime timing is the hardest problem.

The team should consider ensemble methods — averaging predictions across models — to reduce regime-dependent volatility.

Confidence should be dynamically adjusted based on whether macro signals (oil, rates, seasonality) align with technical momentum. In Week 23, they didn't; in Week 24, they did.

Looking ahead to Week 25: The market closed the week higher, with futures pointing to further gains. The key questions are whether the rally has legs, whether the rotation into small caps and semiconductors continues, and how the SpaceX IPO and US-Iran developments unfold.
